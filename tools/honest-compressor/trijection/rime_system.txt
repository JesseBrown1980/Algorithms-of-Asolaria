=== FULL RIME SYSTEM — real Wikipedia bytes, end to end ===
input: 27,000 real enwik bytes  ->  1000 blocks of 27  (rime prism w=951846)

MEASURED (the math, in the open):
  reconstruction byte-exact (lossless)      : True
  source                                    : 27,000 B = 216,000 bits, order-0 entropy 4.9982 bpc
  rime-key: 27,000 coords x ceil(log2 p)=20 bits = 540,000 bits (2.50x the source)
  entropy of the rime-key coordinates       : 14.6432 bits/coord
  -> rime-key at its own entropy            : 49,421 B  vs  source-entropy 16,869 B
  stubbed room on disk                      : 81,000 B

WHAT THE MATH SAYS (honest):
  * The prism is LOSSLESS and REVERSIBLE — byte-exact. That part is real.
  * But the 27-pt NTT over GF(p) WHITENS the data: the coordinates are ~uniform
    in [0,p), entropy 14.64 bits each. The transform does NOT concentrate
    the information — it re-addresses it. Storing the rime key costs MORE than the
    source (2.5x), and even at the coords' own entropy it does not beat
    the source's 5.00 bpc. The prism is an ADDRESSING transform, not a compressor.
  * Compression still comes from MODELING (vc65 = 1.7464 bpc on enwik8). The rime
    system re-addresses losslessly; it does not push real text below its entropy.
