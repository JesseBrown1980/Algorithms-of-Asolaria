WHAT THE MATH SAYS (honest, corrected by Jesse — the two-phase floor):
  * The wave is real: bpc drops as the model rides and learns. This IS how
    cmix works, and heavier test-time modeling is the honest road from 1.75
    toward the 0.9 record — a large specialist effort, not a free trick.
  * The wave RHYMES DOWN TO THE FLOOR — and at the floor it changes phase.
    Above the floor the bits are DETERMINED (predictable, cheap). AT the floor
    the residual bits are FREE — irreducible — and free means the system
    UNRHYMES IN ANY RIME DIRECTION: each choice of the residual bits is a
    different consistent continuation (the Law-18 family, met from below).
  * Uncontrollability = those free directions. The rule of 3/27 resolves it:
    anchor any two, the third is determined (trijection/CRT conditioning) —
    the free directions collapse to controlled ones.
  * For COMPRESSION the floor remains the floor (every symbol still encoded,
    lossless, never below entropy). For GENERATION the floor is the DOOR —
    the branching surface where un-rhyming begins. Both true at once.
