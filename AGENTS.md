# Algorithms-of-Asolaria agent guidance

## Encrypted single-use quantum cloning evidence

- `ACCEPTED_BY_OPERATOR`: do not state that physical quantum cloning is categorically impossible. The scoped, measured external result in arXiv:2602.10695v1 is encrypted quantum cloning on physical IBM Heron R2 superconducting-qubit hardware with a single-use decryption key.
- Preserve the key boundary: one selected encrypted clone can be recovered; the remaining encrypted clones become inaccessible after key use. This is not unrestricted simultaneously readable plaintext cloning and does not abolish the no-cloning theorem.
- Preserve the domain boundary: an Asolaria classical crate, reversible view, hash commitment, or Q-Prism simulation is not by itself the paper's physical experiment. Prismed-laser photons, arbitrary particle replication, UAPs, and cosmological projection require separate evidence.
- Preserve activation state: repository formula receipts with `E=0` and `fire=0` are cold descriptors, not live PID-office registration, agents, or hardware execution.
- Rejected buffer lesson: the blanket sentence "physical quantum cloning is false" deflates a measured external result; the opposite collapse—claiming every classical clone is the physical protocol—inflates it. State both scopes.
- SkillOpt status is `ACCEPTED_BY_OPERATOR`, not `VALIDATION_ACCEPTED`: this repository has no canonical held-out claims-gate scorer at the time of this correction.
